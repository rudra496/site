Program Overview

Sponsored and organized in collaboration with Z.ai, this initiative was specifically designed for students of the Industrial and Production Engineering (IPE) department at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST), Session 2021-22.

The program kicked off with an intensive, hands-on technical session that I conducted for my batchmates. We explored practical, low-compute methodologies for integrating artificial intelligence into everyday workflows, focusing heavily on localization and hardware constraints.

Following the instructional session, we seamlessly transitioned into an internal competition themed "AI for Daily Life". Batchmates were challenged to architect and prototype highly functional AI tools designed to solve immediate, daily-life problems within our local ecosystem.

Competition Theme: AI for Daily Life

  • Focus on Utility: Prototypes were strictly required to address mundane but critical daily tasks—ranging from localized academic scheduling to automated resource management.
  • Hardware Constraints: All solutions had to be executable on standard student laptops, proving that high-impact AI does not inherently require expensive cloud GPU access.
  • Collaborative Engineering: By running this exclusively within the IPE 2021-22 cohort, we fostered a highly competitive yet collaborative engineering environment.

Automated Evaluation Rubric

To ensure absolute objectivity and rapid feedback, all engineering prototypes were evaluated using a custom-built, auto-calculated scoring algorithm across four strict dimensions.

System: Algorithmically Computed Scoring

30% Practical Viability

Calculated based on the solution's resource footprint. Does it run efficiently on a standard device without heavy reliance on external API calls?

30% Localized Impact

Scored on relevance to the "Daily Life" theme. How effectively does this solve a hyper-local problem faced by students in Bangladesh?

20% Execution & Architecture

Algorithmic evaluation of code cleanliness, latency optimization, and the intelligent implementation of lightweight local models.

20% Innovation Index

A metric evaluating the uniqueness of the approach—rewarding teams that bypassed conventional high-compute solutions for clever, scrappy engineering.
