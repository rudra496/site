An initiative dedicated to building practical, low-compute artificial intelligence applications to solve real infrastructure and accessibility challenges in Bangladesh.

Initiative Overview

Designed initially as a campus-wide event, this initiative was strategically pivoted into an intensive internal pilot program. We developed a complete infrastructure—from problem statements to evaluation rubrics—focusing strictly on solutions that can operate effectively within the hardware and resource constraints typical of the Global South. The resulting prototypes demonstrated that profound social impact can be achieved without relying on expensive, high-tier compute clusters.

Core Development Tracks

AI for Accessibility

Development of localized, lightweight AI interfaces designed to bridge the accessibility gap for individuals with disabilities, ensuring tools run efficiently on standard consumer hardware.

Smart Campus & Edge IoT

Integration of distributed sensor networks with edge-level AI inference to autonomously monitor and optimize critical university infrastructure and resource allocation in real-time.

Health & Wellness Tech

Creation of private, localized AI applications tailored to improve health outcomes and well-being without relying on expensive, cloud-dependent commercial APIs.

Evaluation Rubric

The official criteria used to evaluate engineering prototypes during the internal pilot phase.

30% Hardware Practicality

Can the proposed solution run efficiently on standard, low-tier student laptops or mobile devices without requiring cloud GPU access?

30% Localized Impact

Does the solution directly address a verified, specific challenge faced by the Bangladeshi demographic or the immediate university ecosystem?

20% Technical Execution

Evaluation of code architecture, efficiency of API utilization, and intelligent implementation of local models to reduce latency and cost.

20% Value Proposition

The ability of the engineering team to clearly articulate the human value, scalability, and social return on investment of their prototype.
