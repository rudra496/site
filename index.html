Nokia Mobile Sticker Light: The Mystery of Battery-Free Operation!

Nokia button phone sticker lights are not powered by batteries. So how did they work?

Unveiling the Mystery:

Sticker lights work on a principle called the Piezoelectric Effect. When a call or message arrives, the phone's speaker vibrates. This vibration affects a small component called a Piezoelectric crystal. This crystal generates electricity, which lights up the sticker light.

In simple words:

  • When a call or message arrives, the phone speaker vibrates.
  • The vibration affects the Piezoelectric crystal.
  • The Piezoelectric crystal generates electricity.
  • The electricity lights up the sticker light.

Some fun facts:

  • Piezoelectric crystals are used in various electronic devices, such as timers, clocks, gas lighters, etc.
  • Piezoelectric crystals are environmentally friendly because they do not produce waste.
  • In some Nokia phones, the color of the sticker light could be changed.
